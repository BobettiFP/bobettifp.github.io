Hi, I'm **Kim Hyegang** — a PhD student at the [University of Toronto](https://www.utoronto.ca/), working at the intersection of machine learning and complex systems.

## Research Interests

My work focuses on four interconnected areas:

- **Large Language Models (LLMs)** — prompting methods, process automation, and how LLMs can serve as reasoning engines for structured tasks
- **Reinforcement Learning** — algorithm design, theoretical foundations (MDPs, policy gradient, function approximation), and RLHF
- **Network Science** — applying graph-theoretic methods to real-world complex systems
- **Natural Language Processing** — information extraction, event logs, and dialogue systems

## Current Work

I am exploring how LLMs can be leveraged for **process mining** — extracting, conformance-checking, and improving business processes from event logs. This sits at a productive boundary between NLP, sequential decision-making, and object-centric process models (OCPM).

## Background

Before Toronto, I was based in Seoul, South Korea. I arrived at the University of Toronto in the fall of 2025 to pursue doctoral research. Adjusting to a new country has been its own kind of fieldwork — some of which ends up on this blog.

## This Blog

**Scribble Notes** is the open, informal version of my Notion workspace. You will find two kinds of posts here:

- **Research** — summaries, visualizations, and opinions on papers and methods in LLM, RL, NLP, and network science. Written for myself first, hopefully useful to others.
- **Canada Life** — practical notes about moving to Toronto for a PhD: logistics, first impressions, and small observations. Written for anyone about to make the same leap from East Asia.

The name is intentional: these are scribbles, not polished essays. The goal is to think in public.
